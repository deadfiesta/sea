  • Introducing the 3 'S'es

  • Understanding SeaTalk business goals for their consumers

  • Simplifying into 3 keywords by deconstructing SeaTalk’s slogan

    "One-stop for work"

  • Simplicity

    Approachable user design with easy-to-use functionalities.

  • Seamless

    Internal integrations within the app and some degree of external integration with third-party platforms.

  • Sustainable

    Save time and money so businesses can focus theirs resources to iron out business operations for stability and permanence.

    • Effortlessness

      How easy was it to use?

      The need for SeaTalk to be as intuitive as possible is essential as our target audience are average tech consumers who may not be “app-savvy”. The last thing we want is to intimidate our users and drop our app at first sight. Effortlessness should have the highest weight. We want to welcome and motivate our users to dive right in with a design that they’re familiar with. A friendly interface that requires very little guidance or learning curve.

    • Effectiveness

      How well of a job did it do?

      Features in SeaTalk should add value whenever possible. This means giving the users an end result better than their desired expectation of the action performed.

    • Efficiency

      How fast to get the job done?

      The nature of any productivity app like SeaTalk should assist users to accomplish more but with less work in less time. Every action should be seamless; simplified and autonomous, cutting down as many steps as possible.

    Some features may be too similar of a resemblance with competitors. We all know the saying "Don't fix what's not broken" and trying something new carries risk.

  • The Good Stuff

  • Let's keep it that way

  • 01

    Preloading animation

    Loading animation is a wonderful way to convey a brand concept, distract attention when waiting. Why not entertain the user while they wait?

  • 02

    The flow from searching messages to the chat

    The auto focus and the little wiggle at the end is subtle but very helpful to bring attention to the specific chat bubble where the result was found.

  • 03

    Quick reply menu on hover

    Quickly reply to a specific text (especially helpful in group chats). No fuss no muss.

  • 04

    Modal windows for search result and adding new contacts/chat

    Modals are a great way to avoid interrupting the user's workflow from the main window while drawing attention to the new information on screen. Users can multitask and easily return to their original task at hand.

  • 05

    Screenshot region auto snapping

    Modals are a great way to avoid interrupting the user's workflow from the main window while drawing attention to the new information on screen. Users can multitask and easily return to their original task at hand.

  • 06

    Line break tip

    Informing users that they can draft and format their messages instead of sending multiple messages to start on a new line. While this may sound trivial but this tip gives the user the assurance that line breaking is possible in the text field.

  • 07

    Combined forward text sent as chat history object

    Full details are embedded along with the messages. They’re easy to refer to and they don’t flood the chat room unnecessarily.
